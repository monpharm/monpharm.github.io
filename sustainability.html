Introduction

Issues of sustainability and social responsibility hold the utmost importance upon pharmaceutical discovery and development of a disease and sequential drug production. These responsibilities can differ however are mostly closely selected sustainable development goals (SDG) promoted by the United Nations (UN) and followed according to standards set by the International Organisation for standardisation (ISO).

Sustainability Issues - Overseen by UN Goal Number 3

UN’s goal number 3 of ‘Good Health and Well-being’, in particular Target 3.4, aims to reduce mortalities from non-communicable diseases via their treatment and prevention to stimulate good mental health and well-being (7).

Impact of Schizophrenia

Schizophrenia, a non-communicable disorder (11) severely impacts mental well-being of individuals. In absence of good mental health, individuals are unable to perform daily living tasks, which is a risk factor for income and education inequality, nutrition attainment and social exclusion (12). Thoughts of suicide are high in conjunction increased complication in family relationships.

Potential Solutions in Australia

To ensure acquisition of Newloserine is not hindered by financial factors, for Australia it is aimed to listNewloserine on the Pharmaceutical Benefits Scheme (PBS) (1). This allows Australian residents currently holding medicare card an access to a pocket friendly government-subsidised price for the Newloserine (1).

Potential Solutions in Overseas

Ensure availability of Newloserine in pharmacies across the world, wherein country’s respective health department can be contacted to seek schemes such as PBS. Moreover, ensure appropriate advertisement is conducted in foreign countries. Greater access = more treated patients.

Social Responsibility Issues - Impacting UN Goals 12 and 13

Potential social responsibility (SR) issues to be discussed will be managed using ISO2001. This is a voluntary based guidance document which sets standards for SR (4).

Potential Issues due to Working Conditions

The working conditions in which Newloserine is produced can pose a potential risk for physical injury or psychological burden (work related stress). Furthermore, potential hazards in handling modified d-cycloserine are hypothesised from the original d- cycloserine, which include skin, eye or respiratory tract irritations (6).

Solutions for Working Conditions

Following section 6.4.6.3 of ISO 26000 (4), production site would ensure sufficient ventilation and usage of personal protective equipment. Health and safety policy will be introduced, adhering to any risks analysed for workers. Any incidents would be recored, respective prevention strategy would be implemented. Workers to complete a monthly survey using perceived stress scale (2) to manage workload.

Impact on UN Goal 12 and 13

Production of Newloserine would generate wastage from the usage of resources which poses a SR issue and undermine UN goal 12 (8). This could involve disposal of active ingredient - modified d-cycloserine. Production requires energy, and if not obtained from clean energy (wind or solar) can add to rising greenhouse gases impacting goal 13(9).

Simple Solutions to Achieve UN Goals 12 and 13

In order to prevent any negative environmental consequences, calculated quantities of ingredients would be ordered. If any excess is noted, chemical modifications would be conducted to ensure no hazardous materials (ozone depleting substances or organic pollutants) are disposed. Renewable energy (wind and solar) sources would be used to reduce any carbon emissions.
